LOTUS TEMPLE
LOTUS SHRINE
The Lotus Temple in Delhi, inaugurated in 1986, is a Bahá'í House of Worship renowned for its stunning flower-like architecture. Designed by Iranian architect Fariborz Sahba, this marble-clad structure attracts millions of visitors annually for its serene ambiance and universal message of unity and peace.
MUMTAZ MAHAL
TAJ MAHAL
MONUMENT OF LOVE
The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it is renowned for its exquisite architectural beauty and intricate craftsmanship, symbolizing eternal love and one of the world's most admired monuments.
ALAI MINAR
QUTUB MINAR
DHRUV STAMBH
The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at a height of 73 meters. Built in the 12th century by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a remarkable example of Indo-Islamic architecture, featuring intricate carvings and inscriptions.
VICTORY ARCH
INDIA GATE
WAR MEMORIAL
India Gate, located in New Delhi, is a majestic war memorial built in 1931 to honor the soldiers of the British Indian Army who died in World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, this iconic arch stands 42 meters tall and serves as a symbol of national pride and remembrance.
